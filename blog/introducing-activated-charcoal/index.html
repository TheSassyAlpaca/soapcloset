The Soap Closet

Activated Charcoal

Since I started working with activated charcoal I have learned two things: it doesn’t take much to turn your kitchen towels grey and this stuff is NOT for folks that don’t absolutely need it. Whenever I make a soap for the first time I don’t sell any until I have cured a bar and used it for a couple of weeks. Activated charcoal is no joke, I didn’t even make it through the first week. It sucked the oil right out of my hair, face, beard, Rav4, and lawnmower.

Jet

Jet is my strongest activated charcoal soap. Over 33% coconut oil for an extreme clean; tea tree essential oil for its anti-bacterial, anti-fungal, anti-viral properties; goat milk to improve lather; and shea butter to keep you moisturized - very important given activated charcoal’s scorched earth policy regarding your body’s natural oils.

Mercer

Mercer is a dual-layered soap: one layer is the aforementioned Jet while the other is an orange puree soap with orange essential oil. In addition to a citrus scent, the orange pulp gently massages the skin encouraging the restoration of the body’s natural oils to reduce the negative impact that activated charcoal can have.

Dark Crystal

Dark Crystal is half the activated charcoal of Jet with lavender buds. In addition to being a gentle exfoliant, the lavender buds are soaked in lavender essential oil to shield the oil from the activated charcoal during the soap making process and to cause the oil to be expressed onto the skin when the soap is used.
