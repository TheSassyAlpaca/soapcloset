The Soap Closet

Why We Say NO To Mica

I pride myself on using only natural ingredients, but there are some of nature’s gifts that come at too high a price. Mica is one of those ingredients.

What Is Mica?

Mica is a mineral made primarily of silicon and oxygen known for its colors and luster. Historically mica was used in paints and decorative coatings. Today, mica is primarily used in cosmetics such as foundation, eye shadow and eye liner, lipstick, blush, and soap.
About two-thirds of the world’s mica is mined from impoverished regions of India where child-labor laws are largely ignored and debt-bondage is rampant. In addition to mica dust that causes fatal respiratory conditions, the risk of mine-collapse, cuts that often go untreated resulting in infection, the miners, about 20-30% of which are children as young as 5, are in danger of snake, spider, and scorpion bites both in the mines and on the surface. Nearly 100 miners die from conditions every month, about a dozen of which are children.

Mica Is Useless

Mica is a mineral made primarily of silicon and oxygen known for its colors and luster. Historically mica was used in paints and decorative coatings. Today, mica is primarily used in cosmetics such as foundation, eye shadow and eye liner, lipstick, blush, and soap.
In most cosmetics. mica serves a function, it provides the color. In soaps it adds no benefit other than to make the soap ‘perdy’. It does not exfoliate, it does not transfer vital minerals to the skin, it does not improve lather, it is completely useless. Sadly, almost all artisan soap makers continue to use mica in their soaps. What is worse, most consumers don’t even realize that they are funding what is just this side of slavery.
