Local, and Better for It

The Sassy Alpaca is part soapmaking, part traditional medicine, and all experimentation. Buying local is a huge part of that. I network with local growers and artisans to find the best ingredients that middle Georgia has to offer. Here are just a few of my local partners and the soaps that they make possible.

The Rust'd Plow

Johnny at The Rust'd Plow came through for me early this year when he was able to bring me fresh strawberries for my Mema's Garden soap. Mema's Garden uses only fresh strawberries which makes it a seasonal soap. Last year I underestimated how much people, including my wife, would love this soap. Thanks to Johnny, I was able to make and delivere a few batches to some of you that have been asking for it since I ran out in October.

Ernie's Eggs

Ernie of Ernie's Eggs has been helping me find and grow herbs for a couple of years now so there are a number of his herbs that may have found their way into my soaps. The most notable of which is cilantro which is found in my Mojito soap. Not as popular as other fruit & herb soaps such as Mema's Garden or Blueberry Hill, but it has found its niche.

JG's Apiaries

JG brings rich wildflower honey and unfiltered beeswax to a number of products that I make, not just soaps. My Oats & Honey soaps such as Gandolf, my beard balm, my deodorant, and even my new wax melts all benefit from his expertise.

Local Lands

Local Lands has a plethora of fresh vegetables, hand blended teas, and even muscadine! Currently, I have only had the chance to experiment with incorporating their Green Jasmine tea in a soap by the same name, but as soon as muscadines are in season I plan to remedy that. Stay tuned for muscadine soap!

Ava's Apples

Ava's Apples brings us the key ingredient in one of my first challenge soaps. Thanks to Ava's Turmeric blend, I was able to make my Kentucky Fried Papaya.

Oliver Farm

Oliver Farm produces a number of artisan oils, but it's their cold-pressed, unrefined, Pecan oil that makes all of our Southern soaps possible.
