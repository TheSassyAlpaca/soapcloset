About Me

I started making soap on my father’s farm when I was 9. Back then we mixed milk from our goats with tallow rendered from our calves. When we started selling soap at county fairs and flea markets we began adding essential oils such as cedarwood & patchouli and orange & tea tree - two of my favorite scent profiles that I continue to use today. Soon, we began including natural additives such as oats for medicinal benefits as well as exfoliation and honey to improve bar hardness and lather. Over the last 30 years I have experimented with vegetable oils and nut butters as well as herbs and fruits to figure out how best to help people find relief. Today I have three oil recipes (original, vegan, and southern) along with dozens of natural additives such as oats & honey for eczema and psoriasis; silver maple & pine bark for anti-aging and a grittier natural exfoliant; and even fruit purees and zests to balance pH and provide a gentler exfoliant.

Relief for Everyone

All of my soaps use natural ingredients: certified organic oils, therapeutic grade essential oils, and locally sourced, organically grown, natural additives. I refuse to use any fragrance oils, oxides, micas, dyes, or any other artificial ingredients. This can make for a very expensive bar of soap.To offset these high costs I buy ingredients in bulk and sell in bulk. This means that when you find the right bar for you, that you, and your family, can afford to use it.

Here to Stay

Handmade soap is a very popular hobby. Soapers seem to come and go, abandoning customers as they disappear. I, however, am here to stay. Making soap isn’t just a hobby for me, it is what I do and it is a huge part of who I am. It is the only profession that I have ever had that did not make me feel as though my autism was preventing me from being successful. It is also the only skill that I have practiced for over 30 years that constantly makes me feel like there is more worth learning.
